# United Rentals — Policies, Terms & Conditions

## Overview

This document outlines United Rentals' standard rental terms and conditions, customer obligations, damage and insurance policies, return standards, and support channels. ISRs should reference this document when explaining policies to customers, resolving disputes, and ensuring consistent application of UR standards across all transactions.

---

## 1. Rental Period Definition

### 1.1 Rental Period Start and End

The rental period is defined as the time from **"Rental Out"** to **"Scheduled In"**:

- **Rental Out** — The date and time the equipment leaves the United Rentals branch for delivery, or the date and time the customer picks up the equipment from the branch
- **Scheduled In** — The date and time the customer notifies United Rentals that the equipment is ready for pickup, or the date and time the customer returns the equipment to the branch

**Key Clarifications:**

| Scenario | Rental Period Start | Rental Period End |
|---|---|---|
| UR delivers to jobsite | Truck departs branch | Customer calls off-rent + UR picks up equipment |
| Customer picks up at branch | Customer signs rental contract | Equipment returned to branch |
| Will-call (customer pickup, UR delivery back) | Customer picks up at branch | Customer calls off-rent + UR picks up equipment |

### 1.2 Billing Cycles

| Rental Duration | Billing Rate Applied |
|---|---|
| 1–4 hours | Minimum charge (typically 4-hour minimum) |
| 1 day | Daily rate |
| 1 week (7 calendar days) | Weekly rate (typically 3x daily rate) |
| 4 weeks (28 calendar days) | Monthly rate (typically 3x weekly rate) |

- Billing transitions automatically from daily to weekly to monthly as the rental duration crosses each threshold
- Partial periods are billed at the next higher rate tier (e.g., 5 days billed at weekly rate if lower than 5x daily)
- **Shift rates** may be available for customers operating equipment on defined 8-hour or 10-hour shifts (by prior arrangement)

### 1.3 Off-Rent Process

1. Customer contacts the branch or calls 844-873-4948 to request equipment pickup
2. ISR enters the off-rent request in RentalMan with the customer-requested date
3. Billing continues until the equipment is physically picked up by UR or returned by the customer
4. **24-hour off-rent window** — If UR cannot pick up equipment within 24 hours of the customer's call-off, billing stops at the 24-hour mark (unless the delay is caused by customer site access issues)
5. Off-rent confirmation is sent to the customer via email

## 2. Customer Obligations

### 2.1 Equipment Use

Customers are required to:

- **Operate equipment per manufacturer guidelines** — Only trained and authorized operators may use the equipment. Operation must comply with the manufacturer's operating manual, load charts, and safety instructions
- **Use equipment for intended purpose only** — Equipment must not be modified, altered, or used for applications beyond its design specifications
- **Comply with all applicable laws and regulations** — Including OSHA standards, local building codes, environmental regulations, and DOT requirements for road-licensed equipment
- **Provide adequate supervision** — Ensure that equipment is operated under appropriate supervision at all times

### 2.2 Maintenance During Rental

- **Customer responsibility:** Routine daily maintenance as specified in the operator's manual (e.g., fluid level checks, visual inspections, tire pressure, greasing)
- **UR responsibility:** Mechanical repairs, hydraulic system service, and major maintenance beyond routine daily checks
- **Reporting:** Customer must report any malfunction, damage, or unusual equipment behavior to United Rentals **within 24 hours** of discovery
- **Tampering prohibition:** Customer must not attempt repairs, remove safety devices, or modify equipment without written UR authorization

### 2.3 Return Condition

Equipment must be returned in the **same condition as received**, with normal wear and tear excepted:

- **Full fuel tank** — Equipment must be returned with a full tank of the correct fuel type. If returned with less than a full tank, a refueling charge will apply (current refueling rate is posted at the branch)
- **Clean condition** — Equipment must be reasonably clean. Excessive mud, concrete, paint, or other material buildup may result in a cleaning charge
- **All keys and accessories** — All keys, remote controls, manuals, attachments, and accessories provided at the time of rental must be returned. Lost or unreturned items will be charged at replacement cost
- **Secure transport** — If customer is returning equipment to the branch, they are responsible for safe and legal transport

### 2.4 Damage Reporting

- Customer must promptly report any damage, accident, theft, or vandalism to the branch
- A **Damage/Incident Report** must be completed within 24 hours
- Customer must cooperate with UR's investigation, including providing access to the equipment and jobsite
- Customer must not repair damaged equipment without UR authorization
- If equipment is involved in an accident resulting in injury, the customer must notify UR immediately and preserve the scene

### 2.5 Insurance and Risk of Loss

- Equipment is the **customer's responsibility** from Rental Out to Scheduled In (and physical return)
- Customer bears risk of loss for theft, vandalism, weather damage, and third-party damage while in their possession
- Customer must maintain adequate insurance coverage (see Section 4) or purchase UR's Rental Protection Plan (RPP)

## 3. Equipment Return Standards

### 3.1 Return Inspection Process

When equipment is returned (either at the branch or upon UR pickup):

1. **Visual inspection** — UR personnel inspect the equipment for damage, missing components, and cleanliness
2. **Operational check** — Equipment is started and key functions tested
3. **Hour meter reading** — Recorded for billing and maintenance scheduling
4. **Fuel level check** — Fuel level recorded; refueling charge applied if below full
5. **Damage documentation** — Any damage discovered is photographed and documented
6. **Customer notification** — If damage is found, the customer is notified within 48 hours with repair cost estimate

### 3.2 Return Charges

| Condition | Charge |
|---|---|
| Less than full fuel tank | Refueling charge: UR fuel rate × gallons to fill (UR rate typically 15–20% above local pump price) |
| Excessive cleaning required | Cleaning fee: $75–$500 depending on equipment size and contamination level |
| Missing keys | Replacement key charge: $25–$150 per key depending on equipment type |
| Missing attachments/accessories | Replacement cost of the item |
| Flat or damaged tires | Repair or replacement cost |
| Broken or cracked glass | Replacement cost |
| Hydraulic hose or fitting damage | Repair cost (labor + parts) |
| Structural damage (bent arms, frames) | Full repair cost or, if unrepairable, fair market value of equipment |

### 3.3 Damage Dispute Process

If a customer disputes a damage charge:

1. Customer submits a written dispute (email or through Total Control) within **30 days** of receiving the damage invoice
2. UR reviews the pre-rental inspection report and photos (taken at rental out)
3. UR reviews the post-rental inspection report and photos (taken at return)
4. Branch manager reviews and makes initial determination
5. If unresolved, escalated to the regional operations manager
6. Customer is notified of the outcome within 10 business days of dispute submission
7. Approved adjustments are credited to the customer's account

## 4. Insurance Requirements

### 4.1 General Insurance Requirements

All customers renting from United Rentals must provide **one** of the following:

1. **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** meeting UR's minimum requirements, or
2. **Rental Protection Plan (RPP)** purchased through United Rentals

### 4.2 Certificate of Insurance Requirements

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---|---|
| Commercial General Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate |
| Automobile Liability (road-licensed equipment) | $1,000,000 combined single limit |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory limits |
| Inland Marine / Equipment Floater | Full replacement value of rented equipment |
| Additional Insured | United Rentals, Inc. must be named as additional insured |
| Waiver of Subrogation | Required in favor of United Rentals |

**COI Submission:**

- Must be submitted before or at the time of first rental
- COI must be from an insurance carrier rated A- VII or better by AM Best
- Updated COI required annually or upon policy renewal
- Submit via email to the branch or upload through Total Control

### 4.3 Road-Licensed Equipment

Equipment that is licensed for road use (e.g., trucks, trailers, towable equipment) requires:

- Valid automobile liability insurance with $1,000,000 minimum CSL
- Customer must have valid driver's license appropriate for the equipment class
- Customer is responsible for all traffic violations, tolls, and parking citations
- Customer must comply with DOT hours of service regulations (where applicable)
- Equipment may not be taken outside the continental United States without prior written authorization

### 4.4 Off-Road Equipment

For off-road equipment (aerial lifts, earthmoving, material handling, etc.):

- Inland marine or equipment floater policy covering the full replacement value of the equipment
- Coverage must include theft, vandalism, fire, flood, wind, and accidental damage
- Deductible must not exceed $5,000 per occurrence

### 4.5 Rental Protection Plan (RPP)

For customers who do not carry their own insurance or whose coverage does not meet UR minimums:

- **RPP Rate:** Percentage of the rental rate (typically 12–15% of the base rental charge)
- **Coverage:** Physical damage to the rented equipment from covered causes (collision, upset, fire, theft, vandalism, weather)
- **Deductible:** $500 per occurrence for most equipment; $2,500 for equipment valued over $100,000
- **Exclusions:** Intentional damage, operator error/negligence, use outside manufacturer specifications, damage from failure to maintain, mysterious disappearance without evidence of forced entry

| RPP Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Enrollment | At time of rental (cannot be added retroactively) |
| Billing | Added as a line item on each rental invoice |
| Cancellation | May be cancelled with submission of qualifying COI |
| Liability cap | Replacement value of the specific unit |
| Tire and glass coverage | Included for no additional charge |

## 5. Branch Transfer Policies

### 5.1 Customer-Requested Transfers

When a customer needs equipment delivered from a different branch than their home branch:

1. **Availability check** — ISR checks inventory at nearby branches via RentalMan
2. **Transfer authorization** — Receiving branch manager approves the inbound transfer
3. **Transfer charges** — A transfer/mobilization fee may apply based on distance:

| Distance | Typical Transfer Fee |
|---|---|
| 0–25 miles | No additional charge (within delivery zone) |
| 26–50 miles | $150–$350 per unit |
| 51–100 miles | $350–$750 per unit |
| 100+ miles | Custom quote based on equipment size and transport requirements |

4. **Lead time** — Inter-branch transfers typically require 1–3 business days depending on distance and equipment type
5. **Return to originating branch** — Equipment may be returned to the delivering branch or the customer's home branch; customer should confirm at time of rental

### 5.2 UR-Initiated Transfers

United Rentals may transfer equipment between branches for fleet optimization purposes. This does not affect the customer's rental; billing and terms remain with the originating branch contract.

## 10. Frequently Referenced Policies

### 10.1 Minimum Rental Period

- Standard minimum rental: **4 hours** or **1 day** (varies by equipment category)
- Some specialty equipment may have a 1-week minimum rental
- Minimum rental period is specified on the rental contract

### 10.2 Overtime and Weekend Use

- Standard rental rates are based on an **8-hour shift per day**, **40 hours per week**
- Usage beyond the defined shift may incur overtime charges
- Weekend usage: Equipment rented on a daily basis is billed for weekend days unless a weekly rate applies
- **Meter-based billing** may apply to certain equipment categories (charged per hour of engine operation above the included hours)

### 10.3 Equipment Substitution

- If the specific model requested is unavailable, UR may substitute comparable equipment at the same or lower rate
- Customer must be informed of the substitution before delivery
- Substituted equipment must meet or exceed the specifications of the originally requested unit
- Customer has the right to refuse the substitution

### 10.4 Force Majeure

United Rentals is not liable for delays or non-performance caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to:

- Natural disasters (hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, wildfires)
- Pandemics or public health emergencies
- Government orders or restrictions
- Strikes or labor disputes
- Transportation disruptions
- Supply chain interruptions

In force majeure situations, UR will make commercially reasonable efforts to fulfill orders using alternative branches or equipment sources.

### 10.5 Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- All rental agreements are governed by the laws of the state in which the renting branch is located
- Disputes are subject to mandatory arbitration under the American Arbitration Association (AAA) rules, unless otherwise specified in a Master Service Agreement
- Small claims court jurisdiction is preserved for disputes below the applicable state threshold

## 11. ISR Quick Reference: Policy Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Customer Wants to Return Equipment After Hours

> **ISR Action:** Inform the customer that equipment can be returned to the branch yard during non-business hours if the branch has a secure after-hours return area. The customer should park the equipment in the designated area, leave the keys in the key drop box, and call the branch or 844-873-4948 to confirm the off-rent. Billing continues until the return is verified the next business day.

### Scenario 2: Customer Disputes a Damage Charge

> **ISR Action:** Pull the pre-rental and post-rental inspection reports and photos. Compare the documented condition. If the damage pre-existed, credit the charge. If the damage occurred during the rental, explain the charge and reference the rental contract terms. If the customer remains unsatisfied, escalate to the branch manager within 48 hours.

### Scenario 3: Customer Asks About Insurance Requirements

> **ISR Action:** Explain that UR requires either a qualifying Certificate of Insurance or the Rental Protection Plan. Provide the COI requirements checklist. If the customer wants RPP, add it to the rental contract at the time of rental. Remind the customer that RPP cannot be added retroactively after equipment leaves the branch.

### Scenario 4: Customer Wants to Extend a Rental

> **ISR Action:** Check the customer's account status (credit availability, insurance currency). Extend the rental in RentalMan with the new estimated return date. Notify the customer of any rate tier changes (e.g., daily to weekly). Send updated rental confirmation via email.

### Scenario 5: Customer Reports Equipment Theft

> **ISR Action:** Instruct the customer to file a police report immediately and obtain the report number. Complete the UR Damage/Incident Report. Notify the branch manager and the UR insurance/risk management team. If the customer has RPP, initiate the RPP claim process. If the customer has their own insurance, request the claim number and adjuster contact information. Do not remove the equipment from the rental contract until the claim is resolved.

### Scenario 6: Customer Asks About Fuel Charges

> **ISR Action:** Explain that equipment must be returned with a full fuel tank. If the customer is unable to refuel, UR will refuel and charge at the posted UR fuel rate (available at the branch). Recommend the customer refuel before return to avoid the premium. Provide the correct fuel type for the specific equipment.
