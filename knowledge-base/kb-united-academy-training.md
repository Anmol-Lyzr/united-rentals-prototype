# United Rentals — United Academy Training Programs

## Overview

United Academy is the construction and industrial equipment industry's largest training provider, having trained over 730,000 individuals across North America. With more than 100 full-time certified trainers and over a decade of operational excellence, United Academy delivers OSHA-compliant, ANSI-accredited, and manufacturer-aligned safety and equipment operation training to United Rentals customers, their employees, and the broader workforce.

## 2. Equipment Operation Courses

### 2.1 Aerial Boom Lift & Scissor Lift

**Course Code:** UA-MEWP-100

| Detail | Specification |
|---|---|
| Duration | 4 hours classroom + practical evaluation |
| Prerequisites | Minimum 18 years of age; employer authorization |
| Standards | ANSI A92.22, A92.24 (2020); OSHA 1926.453; IPAF affiliated |
| Certification validity | 3 years from date of completion |
| Class size | Maximum 12 participants (1 instructor) |
| Passing score | 70% minimum on written theory exam |

**Curriculum:**

1. **Module 1: Regulatory Framework (45 min)**
   - OSHA aerial lift standards and employer responsibilities
   - ANSI 2020 updates: familiarization vs. training vs. operator duties
   - Incident statistics and root cause analysis
   - Employee and employer obligations under OSHA

2. **Module 2: Equipment Familiarization (60 min)**
   - Types of MEWPs: telescopic boom, articulating boom, scissor lift, vertical mast
   - Component identification: platform controls, ground controls, emergency lowering
   - Load capacity charts and nameplate information
   - Manufacturer-specific features and restrictions

3. **Module 3: Pre-Operation Inspection (30 min)**
   - Daily inspection checklist walkthrough
   - Identifying defects: structural, hydraulic, electrical, tire/wheel
   - Documentation requirements and "Do Not Operate" tagging
   - Reporting procedures for discovered defects

4. **Module 4: Safe Operation Procedures (45 min)**
   - Ground conditions assessment and setup
   - Travel procedures (stowed vs. elevated)
   - Wind speed limitations and weather considerations
   - Working near power lines: 10-foot rule and utility coordination
   - Fall protection requirements: harness and lanyard use in boom lifts

5. **Module 5: Practical Evaluation (60 min)**
   - Pre-operation inspection demonstration
   - Controlled lift and positioning exercises
   - Obstacle navigation course
   - Emergency descent procedure
   - Instructor sign-off on competency

**Certification Issued:** United Academy MEWP Operator Certificate (wallet card and digital certificate via Total Control)

## 3. Additional OSHA Safety Training

### 3.1 Fall Protection

**Course Code:** UA-FP-500

- **Duration:** 4 hours (classroom) or 8 hours (classroom + hands-on)
- **Content:** OSHA 1926 Subpart M, personal fall protection systems (harnesses, lanyards, SRLs, anchors), fall hazard assessment, rescue planning, equipment inspection
- **Audience:** Workers performing tasks at heights of 6 feet or more (construction) or 4 feet (general industry)
- **Includes:** Hands-on harness donning, anchor point identification, and equipment inspection exercises

### 3.2 Confined Space Entry & Rescue

**Course Code:** UA-CSE-510

- **Duration:** 8 hours (full day)
- **Content:** OSHA 1910.146 and 1926 Subpart AA, confined space identification and classification, atmospheric testing (LEL, O2, H2S, CO), entry permits, attendant duties, rescue procedures and equipment
- **Audience:** Entrants, attendants, entry supervisors, and rescue team members
- **Special equipment:** 4-gas monitors, tripod rescue systems, SCBA awareness

### 3.3 Competent Person: Trenching & Excavation

**Course Code:** UA-CPT-520

- **Duration:** 8 hours (full day)
- **Content:** OSHA 1926 Subpart P, competent person duties and authority, soil classification and testing (visual and manual methods), protective system selection (sloping, benching, shoring, shielding), daily inspection requirements
- **Audience:** Supervisors and foremen responsible for excavation operations
- **Certification:** Competent Person designation card (employer-specific)

### 3.4 General Jobsite Safety (OSHA 10/30 Preparation)

**Course Code:** UA-GJS-530

- **Duration:** 4 hours (overview) or 8 hours (comprehensive)
- **Content:** Focus Four hazards (falls, struck-by, caught-in/between, electrocution), PPE selection and use, hazard communication (GHS/SDS), fire prevention, housekeeping, emergency action plans
- **Note:** This course supplements but does not replace the OSHA 10-hour or 30-hour outreach programs

### 3.5 Train the Trainer

**Course Code:** UA-TTT-600

- **Duration:** 16 hours (2 days)
- **Content:** Adult learning principles, instructional techniques, practical teaching exercises, evaluation methods, documentation requirements, regulatory update processes
- **Audience:** Customer safety directors and designated trainers who will conduct in-house equipment training
- **Prerequisites:** Current operator certification in the relevant equipment category + 2 years of documented operating experience
- **Outcome:** Participants are qualified to conduct operator training within their organization using United Academy curriculum materials (licensed)

## 4. Delivery Methods

### 4.1 Classroom Training

- Conducted at United Rentals branch locations or United Academy training centers
- Instructor-led with multimedia presentations, group exercises, and hands-on practical
- Equipment provided for practical evaluations
- Maximum class sizes enforced per course specifications

### 4.2 Blended Learning

- Online theory modules completed at the student's own pace (typically 1–2 hours)
- Followed by shortened in-person practical evaluation session (typically 1–2 hours)
- Theory must be completed within 14 days prior to the practical session
- Reduces jobsite time away from work by approximately 40%

### 4.3 eLearning (Theory Only)

- Fully online self-paced modules for theory-only courses
- Available 24/7 through the United Academy Learning Management System (LMS)
- Courses include: Hazard Communication, PPE Awareness, Silica Dust Awareness, Heat Illness Prevention
- Completion certificates issued upon passing the online assessment
- **Note:** Equipment operation courses always require an in-person practical evaluation and cannot be completed solely via eLearning

### 4.4 Virtual Instructor-Led Training (VILT)

- Live instructor-led sessions delivered via video conference (Microsoft Teams or Zoom)
- Suitable for theory portions of equipment operation courses and all safety awareness courses
- Interactive: real-time Q&A, breakout rooms, polls, and quizzes
- Camera-on policy required for attendance verification

### 4.5 Onsite Training at Customer Locations

- United Academy trainers travel to the customer's jobsite or facility
- Training conducted on the customer's own equipment or UR-supplied equipment
- Customized to site-specific hazards and equipment configurations
- Minimum participant requirements apply (typically 6 participants per session)
- Scheduling: requires 2 weeks advance notice; 1 week for expedited requests (surcharge may apply)
- Available at all 486+ United Rentals locations across North America

## 5. Certification Details

### 5.1 Certification Validity

- All equipment operation certifications are valid for **3 years** from the date of completion
- Refresher training is required before certification expiration to maintain continuous compliance
- Early refresher training (within 6 months of expiration) extends the certification by 3 years from the original expiration date

### 5.2 Passing Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Written theory exam | 70% minimum score required |
| Practical evaluation | Pass/fail based on instructor observation using standardized checklist |
| Attendance | 100% attendance required for full course duration |
| Retake policy | One same-day retake permitted for failed theory exam; practical failures require re-enrollment |

### 5.3 Certification Records

- Digital certificates accessible through Total Control and the United Academy LMS
- Physical wallet cards issued at the time of training completion
- Employer notification: completion reports emailed to the sponsoring company's designated contact
- Records retained by United Academy for a minimum of 5 years
- Verification: third parties may verify certification status by contacting United Academy at 844-873-4948

### 5.4 Certification Renewal Reminders

- Automated email reminders sent at 90 days, 60 days, and 30 days before expiration
- Total Control dashboard displays upcoming certification expirations for all trained employees
- ISRs can generate certification status reports for customer accounts on request

## 8. ISR Quick Reference: Training Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Customer Asks About Available Training

> **ISR Action:** Ask the customer what equipment their operators need to be trained on. Check the United Academy scheduling system for upcoming courses at the nearest branch. Provide dates, times, pricing, and registration instructions. Offer to register participants on the spot.

### Scenario 2: Customer Needs Training Before Equipment Delivery

> **ISR Action:** Coordinate with the United Academy scheduler to align training dates with the equipment delivery schedule. Recommend blended learning to minimize jobsite downtime. Confirm that the training will be completed and certifications issued before the delivery date.

### Scenario 3: Customer Wants Onsite Training for a Large Crew

> **ISR Action:** Confirm the number of participants, equipment types, and preferred dates. Submit an onsite training request to the United Academy coordinator. Quote includes trainer travel, per-participant fees, and equipment supply (if UR provides the training equipment). Provide a formal proposal within 48 hours.

### Scenario 4: Customer's Operators Have Expired Certifications

> **ISR Action:** Pull the certification status report from Total Control (if available) or request from United Academy. Notify the customer of expired certifications and OSHA compliance implications. Schedule refresher courses immediately. If equipment is currently on rent, advise the customer that uncertified operators should not operate until recertified.

### Scenario 5: Customer Asks If Their Existing Training Qualifies

> **ISR Action:** Explain that OSHA requires employer-specific training and familiarization. Prior training from other providers may satisfy the general knowledge requirement, but United Rentals recommends equipment-specific familiarization at minimum. Offer the United Academy familiarization module as a supplementary option.
