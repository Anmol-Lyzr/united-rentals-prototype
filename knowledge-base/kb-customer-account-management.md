# United Rentals — Customer Account Management

## Overview

United Rentals serves over 240,000 active customer accounts across North America through its network of 1,600+ branch locations. Account management encompasses new account creation, credit management, jobsite administration, authorized user controls, and self-service digital platforms including Total Control and the UR Mobile App.

---

## 1. New Account Setup

### 1.1 Walk-In Account Creation

When a customer visits a United Rentals branch in person to open a new account:

1. **Greet and identify the customer's needs** — Determine whether the customer is an individual, sole proprietor, or representative of a business entity.
2. **Collect required documentation:**

| Customer Type | Required Documents |
|---|---|
| Individual / Sole Proprietor | Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport), proof of address (utility bill or bank statement dated within 90 days) |
| LLC / Corporation | Articles of Incorporation or Certificate of Formation, EIN verification letter (IRS CP 575 or equivalent), authorized signer ID |
| Partnership | Partnership agreement, EIN verification, authorized signer ID |
| Government / Municipal | Purchase order or authorization letter on agency letterhead, tax-exempt certificate (if applicable) |

3. **Complete the Credit Application** — The customer fills out the UR Credit Application form, which includes:
   - Legal business name and DBA (if applicable)
   - Federal Tax ID / EIN (or SSN for sole proprietors)
   - Business address, mailing address, and billing address
   - Three trade references with account numbers and contact info
   - Bank reference (institution name, account number, contact)
   - Requested credit limit
   - Authorized signers and their titles
4. **Submit for credit review** — The branch ISR submits the application to the UR Credit Department. Standard processing time is 24–48 business hours. Expedited review is available for national account referrals.
5. **Interim rental options** — While credit is being reviewed, the customer may rent on a COD (Cash on Delivery) basis using a valid credit card or certified funds.

### 1.2 Call-In Account Creation

For customers calling a branch or the national customer care line (844-873-4948):

1. **Verify caller identity** — Confirm the caller's full name, title, and company affiliation.
2. **Email the Credit Application** — Send the UR Credit Application PDF or direct the customer to the online application at unitedrentals.com/credit-application.
3. **Collect documentation electronically** — Accept scanned copies of required documents via email to the branch or through the secure document upload portal.
4. **Phone verification** — The Credit Department may conduct a phone verification call to the business's listed phone number to confirm the application.
5. **Account activation notification** — Once approved, the customer receives an email confirmation with their new account number, assigned branch, and credit terms.

### 1.3 Online Account Creation

Customers can also initiate account setup through unitedrentals.com:

1. Navigate to "Open an Account" on the website
2. Complete the digital credit application form
3. Upload supporting documentation
4. E-sign the rental agreement and terms of service
5. Receive confirmation via email upon approval

## 2. Account Types

### 2.1 National Accounts

National accounts are designed for large enterprises with multi-location operations requiring equipment across multiple United Rentals branches.

**Key Features:**

- **Negotiated rate structures** — Custom pricing tiers based on annual rental volume commitments, typically 10–30% below standard branch rates depending on equipment category and volume
- **Dedicated account representatives** — Assigned National Account Manager (NAM) and regional support team
- **Multi-branch access** — Rent from any UR branch under a single master account with consolidated billing
- **Centralized billing** — Single monthly invoice consolidating all branch-level transactions, with customizable PO and cost center coding
- **Volume rebate programs** — Quarterly or annual rebates based on rental spend thresholds
- **Priority equipment availability** — Advance reservation privileges and fleet pre-positioning for planned projects
- **Custom reporting** — Tailored utilization, spend, and compliance reports via Total Control
- **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** — Governs all rental transactions across the enterprise

**National Account Tiers:**

| Tier | Annual Spend | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Platinum | $5M+ | Dedicated NAM, custom SLAs, executive sponsor, quarterly business reviews |
| Gold | $1M–$5M | Dedicated NAM, priority delivery, monthly reporting |
| Silver | $250K–$1M | Regional account support, consolidated billing, standard reporting |

### 2.2 Local / Branch Accounts

Local accounts are managed at the individual branch level and are suitable for small to mid-sized contractors and businesses.

**Key Features:**

- **Branch-specific pricing** — Rates set by the local branch based on market conditions and competitive landscape
- **Local credit approval** — Branch manager may approve credit up to a defined threshold (typically $5,000–$25,000 depending on branch authorization level)
- **Relationship-based service** — Direct relationship with branch staff, outside sales representatives, and ISRs
- **Standard payment terms** — Net 30 upon credit approval; Net 15 available for smaller accounts
- **Branch-level reporting** — Standard rental history and invoice reports

### 2.3 Walk-In / COD Accounts

For customers who do not require or do not qualify for credit terms:

- **Payment at time of rental** — Full estimated rental charges collected at pickup or delivery
- **Accepted payment methods** — Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, certified check, cash (varies by branch)
- **Security deposit** — May be required; typically 100–150% of the estimated rental value
- **Rental duration limits** — COD rentals may be limited to 30 days without credit review
- **Conversion to credit account** — COD customers with positive rental history may apply for credit terms after establishing a track record (typically 3+ successful rentals)

## 3. Credit Limit Management

### 3.1 Initial Credit Limit Assignment

Credit limits are determined by the UR Credit Department based on:

- Business financial statements (if provided)
- Trade reference feedback (minimum 3 references required)
- Commercial credit bureau scores (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business)
- Industry risk assessment
- Requested limit vs. projected rental needs

### 3.2 Credit Limit Increases

Customers may request a credit limit increase through:

1. **Branch ISR request** — The ISR submits a credit increase request form to the Credit Department
2. **Supporting documentation** — Recent financial statements, updated trade references, or proof of a large upcoming project may accelerate approval
3. **Processing time** — Standard credit increase requests are processed within 24–48 business hours
4. **Automatic reviews** — Accounts with consistent payment history are reviewed annually for potential automatic credit limit increases

### 3.3 Credit Limit Monitoring

| Threshold | Action |
|---|---|
| 75% of credit limit utilized | ISR receives system notification; proactive outreach to customer recommended |
| 90% of credit limit utilized | ISR alert; new rentals may require branch manager approval |
| 100% of credit limit reached | Account auto-hold for new rentals; existing rentals unaffected |
| Over limit with approval | Branch manager or credit manager may grant temporary over-limit authorization for up to 30 days |

## 4. Jobsite Management

### 4.1 Adding and Removing Jobsites

Each customer account can have multiple active jobsites. Jobsite management is critical for accurate delivery, billing, and equipment tracking.

**To add a new jobsite:**

1. Collect the jobsite name, full address (including cross streets if no formal address), and GPS coordinates (if available)
2. Identify the jobsite contact person, phone number, and email
3. Record delivery-specific instructions (gate codes, check-in procedures, equipment placement preferences)
4. Assign a jobsite code or project number for billing reference
5. Enter the jobsite in the RentalMan system and associate it with the customer account

**To deactivate a jobsite:**

1. Confirm all equipment has been returned or transferred from the jobsite
2. Verify no outstanding invoices are tied to the jobsite
3. Mark the jobsite as inactive in RentalMan (do not delete; retain for historical records)
4. Notify the customer of the deactivation

### 4.2 Jobsite-Specific Delivery Instructions

Delivery instructions are stored at the jobsite level and automatically populate on delivery tickets:

- **Access hours** — e.g., "Deliveries accepted Monday–Friday 6:00 AM – 4:00 PM only"
- **Gate codes and security protocols** — e.g., "Gate code #4521, check in at guard shack with photo ID"
- **Equipment placement** — e.g., "Stage equipment at northeast corner of lot, adjacent to trailer #3"
- **Contact on arrival** — e.g., "Call site superintendent John Davis at 555-0147 upon arrival"
- **Special requirements** — e.g., "OSHA 30 certification required for all delivery personnel entering active construction zone"

### 4.3 Total Control Jobsite Tracking

Customers with Total Control access can manage jobsites through the digital platform:

- View all active jobsites on an interactive map
- See real-time equipment inventory at each jobsite
- Track equipment utilization rates by jobsite
- Generate jobsite-specific cost reports
- Set up automated equipment return reminders based on project timelines

## 5. Contact and Authorized User Management

### 5.1 Adding Authorized Users

Only designated authorized users may place orders, request pickups, or make account changes. Managing authorized users properly prevents unauthorized rentals and protects the customer.

**Required information for each authorized user:**

- Full legal name
- Title / role
- Phone number (mobile preferred for delivery notifications)
- Email address
- Authorization level (full account access, rental-only, reporting-only)
- Photo ID on file (recommended for high-value accounts)

### 5.2 Authorization Levels

| Level | Permissions |
|---|---|
| Full Account Administrator | Place/modify/cancel orders, approve invoices, manage users, change account settings, view all reports |
| Rental Manager | Place/modify/cancel orders, view invoices, view rental reports |
| Jobsite Supervisor | Place orders for assigned jobsite(s) only, confirm deliveries/pickups |
| View Only | View rental history, invoices, and reports; no transactional authority |

### 5.3 Removing Authorized Users

When a customer requests removal of an authorized user:

1. Verify the request comes from an account administrator or primary contact
2. Immediately deactivate the user in RentalMan
3. Revoke Total Control access (if applicable)
4. Document the removal with date, requestor name, and reason
5. Confirm the removal with the account administrator via email

## 6. Account Holds and Reinstatement

### 6.1 Reasons for Account Holds

| Hold Type | Trigger | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Credit Hold | Past-due balance exceeding 60 days or credit limit exceeded | No new rentals; existing rentals continue |
| Collections Hold | Account referred to collections (90+ days past due) | No new rentals; existing rentals may be recalled |
| Insurance Hold | Expired or insufficient insurance documentation | No new rentals until updated insurance on file |
| Safety Hold | Safety violation reported at a UR-supplied jobsite | No new rentals pending safety review |
| Customer-Requested Hold | Customer requests temporary account suspension | No new rentals; no billing on existing equipment (must be returned) |

### 6.2 Reinstatement Process

1. **Identify the hold reason** — Review the account status in RentalMan
2. **Resolve the underlying issue:**
   - Credit Hold: Customer must bring balance current or make approved payment arrangement
   - Insurance Hold: Customer must provide updated Certificate of Insurance meeting UR requirements
   - Safety Hold: Safety review must be completed and corrective actions documented
3. **Credit Department approval** — For credit and collections holds, the Credit Department must authorize reinstatement
4. **Reactivate the account** — Remove the hold flag in RentalMan and notify the customer
5. **Document the reinstatement** — Record the date, authorizing party, and resolution details

## 7. Total Control Platform

### 7.1 Overview

Total Control is United Rentals' proprietary customer self-service digital platform, providing comprehensive fleet visibility, financial management, and operational analytics. It is the industry's most advanced equipment management platform.

### 7.2 Core Capabilities

**Fleet Visibility:**

- Real-time location tracking for all rented equipment
- Equipment status dashboard (on-rent, scheduled for delivery, scheduled for pickup)
- Telematics data from 375,000+ equipped units across the UR fleet
- Engine hours, fuel levels, and idle time monitoring
- Geofencing alerts when equipment moves outside designated jobsite boundaries

**Invoicing and Financial Management:**

- View and download invoices in real-time
- Invoice dispute submission and tracking
- Payment history and aging reports
- Budget tracking by project, cost center, or PO number
- Consolidated billing views for national accounts
- Export capabilities for integration with customer ERP systems (SAP, Oracle, QuickBooks)

**Equipment Utilization Analytics:**

- Utilization rate dashboards by equipment type, jobsite, and time period
- Cost-per-hour analysis for rented equipment
- Recommendations for right-sizing fleet based on utilization patterns
- Comparison benchmarks against industry averages
- Idle equipment identification and automated return recommendations

**Emissions Tracking:**

- CO2 equivalent emissions reporting by equipment category
- Emissions trend analysis over time
- Comparison of Tier 4 Final vs. older equipment emissions impact
- Support for customer sustainability reporting requirements (CDP, GRI, TCFD)
- Equipment search filters for lowest-emission options

**Lien Release Management:**

- Request and track lien releases digitally
- Automated lien release generation upon payment confirmation
- Conditional and unconditional lien release support
- Download and share lien releases with project stakeholders

**Notification Configuration:**

- Email and SMS alerts for delivery and pickup confirmations
- Invoice availability notifications
- Credit limit threshold warnings
- Equipment maintenance alerts (from telematics data)
- Custom notification rules based on account-specific triggers

### 7.3 Total Control Access Setup

To set up a customer on Total Control:

1. Verify the customer has an active UR account in good standing
2. Navigate to the Total Control administration panel in RentalMan
3. Enter the user's email address and assign a role (Admin, Manager, Viewer)
4. The system sends an automated invitation email with login credentials
5. The customer completes registration, sets a password, and configures notification preferences
6. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all Total Control accounts

## 9. ISR Quick Reference: Common Account Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Customer Wants to Open a New Account by Phone

> **ISR Action:** Collect company name, contact info, and email. Send the credit application via email. Inform the customer that processing takes 24–48 hours. Offer COD rental as an interim option if they need equipment immediately.

### Scenario 2: Customer Hits Credit Limit During Active Project

> **ISR Action:** Check the account aging. If current on payments, submit a temporary credit increase request to the Credit Department with justification (project details, expected spend). Follow up within 4 hours. If approved, notify customer and update RentalMan.

### Scenario 3: Customer Wants to Add a New Jobsite

> **ISR Action:** Collect jobsite address, contact person, phone, and delivery instructions. Create the jobsite in RentalMan and associate with the account. Confirm the jobsite code with the customer for future reference.

### Scenario 4: Customer Reports Unauthorized Rental on Their Account

> **ISR Action:** Immediately place a temporary hold on the account. Verify the current authorized user list with the primary account contact. Remove any unauthorized users. Escalate to the branch manager if fraudulent activity is suspected.

### Scenario 5: Customer Needs Help Accessing Total Control

> **ISR Action:** Verify the customer's email on file. Check if an invitation was sent. If not, send a new invitation from the Total Control admin panel. Walk the customer through the registration process. If login issues persist, reset the password or escalate to UR IT Support.
