## 1. Billing Overview

United Rentals invoices customers based on the actual rental duration using the most favorable rate tier (daily, weekly, or monthly). Invoices are generated upon equipment return (off-rent) or on a periodic billing cycle for long-term rentals.

### 1.1 Billing Cycle

| Customer Type | Billing Frequency |
|---|---|
| COD / Walk-In | At time of rental (prepaid) + final settlement at return |
| Local Credit Account | Weekly or bi-weekly during active rental; final invoice at off-rent |
| National Account | Per NA agreement — typically weekly or monthly consolidated |

### 1.2 Invoice Components

A standard UR invoice includes the following line items:

| Line Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Equipment Rental | Base rental charge calculated by rate tier (daily/weekly/monthly) |
| Rental Protection Plan (RPP) | 15% of rental charges (if elected) |
| Environmental Service Charge | Mandatory fee for environmental compliance (see Section 3) |
| Delivery Fee | One-way charge for equipment delivery to jobsite |
| Pickup Fee | One-way charge for equipment pickup from jobsite |
| Fuel Convenience Charge | Charged if equipment is returned with less than a full fuel tank |
| Cleaning Charge | Charged if equipment requires excessive cleaning upon return |
| Damage Charges | Repair or replacement costs for damage beyond normal wear (may be offset by RPP) |
| Missing Items | Charges for unreturned keys, manuals, accessories, or attachments |
| Taxes | Applicable state, county, and local sales/use taxes |

## 2. Rental Protection Plan (RPP)

### 2.1 What RPP Is

The Rental Protection Plan (RPP) is a **damage waiver program** — it is **NOT insurance**. RPP is a contractual agreement between United Rentals and the customer that limits the customer's financial liability for accidental loss or damage to rented equipment.

### 2.2 RPP Cost

- **15% of the base rental charges** (applied to the equipment rental amount only, not to delivery, fuel, or other fees).
- RPP is calculated automatically by RentalMan when elected.
- RPP appears as a separate line item on quotes, contracts, and invoices.

### 2.3 RPP Coverage — What Is Covered

When RPP is purchased, the customer's liability for accidental damage or loss to the rented equipment is limited to the **lesser of:**

| Liability Cap | Calculation |
|---|---|
| 10% of the replacement value of the equipment | Based on UR's current replacement cost |
| 10% of the repair cost | Based on actual repair invoice |
| $500 | Flat minimum deductible |

**The customer pays the LEAST of these three amounts**, not all three.

**Examples:**

| Scenario | Equipment Value | Repair Cost | Customer Pays (with RPP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minor hydraulic hose damage | $75,000 | $800 | $80 (10% of repair) |
| Moderate boom damage | $150,000 | $12,000 | $500 (flat minimum) |
| Total loss (theft/fire) | $200,000 | N/A (replacement) | $500 (flat minimum, since 10% of replacement = $20,000 but $500 is lesser) |
| Windshield crack | $50,000 | $400 | $40 (10% of repair) |

> **Important:** Without RPP, the customer is responsible for 100% of the repair or replacement cost.

### 2.4 RPP Coverage — What Is NOT Covered

RPP does **not** cover the following:

| Exclusion | Details |
|---|---|
| Tire blowouts and punctures | Tires are excluded from RPP coverage **EXCEPT** for vehicles rented for 60 days or fewer. For rentals ≤60 days, tire damage IS covered by RPP. For rentals >60 days, tire damage is the customer's responsibility regardless of RPP. |
| Intentional abuse or misuse | Damage resulting from deliberate misuse, overloading beyond rated capacity, unauthorized modifications, or using equipment for purposes it was not designed for. |
| Operation by uncertified/untrained personnel | Damage caused by operators who lack required certifications (e.g., operating a boom lift without aerial certification). |
| Consequential damages | Lost productivity, project delays, third-party property damage, or bodily injury are not covered by RPP. |
| Criminal activity | Damage or loss resulting from criminal acts by the customer or their agents. |
| War, nuclear, terrorism | Standard force majeure exclusions. |

### 2.5 RPP Purchase Rules

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| **Must be elected at rental start** | RPP must be purchased when the rental contract is created. It **cannot** be added mid-rental. |
| **Cannot be cancelled mid-rental** | Once elected, RPP remains in effect for the entire rental period. |
| **Applies per contract line** | RPP can be elected for some equipment on a contract and declined for others (per line item). |
| **Decline requires proof of insurance** | If the customer declines RPP, they must provide a valid Certificate of Insurance (COI) naming United Rentals as additional insured and loss payee, with coverage adequate for the equipment's replacement value. |

### 2.6 RPP vs. Customer's Own Insurance

| Factor | RPP | Customer's Insurance |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | 15% of rental charges (predictable, pay-as-you-go) | Varies (deductibles, premiums, coverage gaps) |
| Simplicity | UR handles the claim internally | Customer files with their carrier; UR pursues subrogation |
| Deductible | Capped at lesser of 10%/10%/$500 | Customer's policy deductible (often $1,000–$10,000+) |
| Tire coverage (≤60 days) | Included | Depends on policy |
| Administrative burden | None for customer | Customer manages the claim |

> **ISR Guidance:** Always present RPP as a value-add. For short-term rentals and customers without strong insurance programs, RPP is typically the most cost-effective protection. Use the comparison table above when discussing with customers.

### 2.7 RPP Claims Process

1. Customer reports damage or loss to the branch or ISR.
2. Branch documents the damage with photos and a written description.
3. UR estimates the repair cost or determines replacement value.
4. UR calculates the customer's RPP liability (lesser of 10% replacement, 10% repair, or $500).
5. Customer is invoiced for their RPP liability amount.
6. UR absorbs the remaining cost.

## 3. Environmental Service Charge (ESC)

### 3.1 What It Is

The Environmental Service Charge is a **mandatory fee** applied to all rental transactions. It covers United Rentals' costs for compliance with federal, state, and local environmental regulations, including:

- Proper disposal of fluids (hydraulic oil, engine oil, coolant, fuel)
- Emissions compliance and testing
- Spill prevention and containment
- Hazardous material handling and documentation
- Environmental audits and reporting

### 3.2 How It Is Calculated

- ESC is a **percentage of the base rental charge** (percentage varies by state/jurisdiction).
- ESC is automatically applied by RentalMan — ISRs do not manually add or adjust it.
- ESC is **not negotiable** and **cannot be waived** (it is a cost-recovery charge, not a profit center).

### 3.3 ISR Talking Points

When a customer questions the ESC:
- "The Environmental Service Charge covers our compliance with federal and state environmental regulations for equipment maintenance, fluid disposal, and emissions standards."
- "This charge applies to all customers equally and is not negotiable."
- "It ensures that the equipment you receive meets all environmental standards and that we handle maintenance fluids responsibly."

## 4. Fuel Convenience Charge (FCC)

### 4.1 Policy

All fuel-powered rental equipment (diesel, gasoline, propane) is dispatched with a **full fuel tank**. The customer is expected to return equipment with a full tank.

### 4.2 How It Works

| Return Condition | Charge |
|---|---|
| Full tank | No fuel charge |
| Less than full tank | Fuel Convenience Charge applies |

### 4.3 Rate

- The Fuel Convenience Charge is billed at the **per-gallon retail rate** for the fuel type (diesel or gasoline), as established by the branch.
- The rate is typically at or slightly above local retail pump prices.
- The charge is calculated based on the volume of fuel needed to refill the tank to full.

### 4.4 ISR Guidance

- Remind customers at rental start and at off-rent call: "Please return the equipment with a full fuel tank to avoid a fuel convenience charge."
- If a customer disputes the FCC, confirm the return fuel level (documented by branch at check-in) and explain the policy.
- For long-term rentals where on-site fueling is impractical, some National Accounts negotiate fuel management terms — check the NA agreement.

## 5. Cleaning Charge

### 5.1 Policy

Equipment should be returned in **reasonably clean condition**, free of excessive mud, debris, concrete, paint, or other materials.

### 5.2 What Triggers a Cleaning Charge

| Condition | Charge? |
|---|---|
| Light dust and normal jobsite grime | No charge |
| Moderate mud on undercarriage/tracks | No charge (normal wear) |
| Excessive mud/clay requiring pressure washing | Cleaning charge applies |
| Concrete or grout on surfaces | Cleaning charge applies |
| Paint overspray on cab/glass | Cleaning charge applies |
| Hazardous material contamination | Cleaning charge + potential hazmat remediation |

### 5.3 Rates

- Cleaning charges vary by equipment size and severity.
- Typical range: $50–$500 depending on equipment type and cleaning effort required.
- Branch documents the condition with photos at return.

## 6. Delivery & Pickup Fees

### 6.1 Structure

- Delivery and pickup are charged as **separate one-way fees**.
- Fees are based on:
  - Distance from the branch to the jobsite (mileage)
  - Equipment size and type (determines transport vehicle)
  - Special transport requirements (pilot cars, permits for oversized loads)

### 6.2 Typical Fee Ranges

| Equipment Category | Typical Delivery Fee Range | Transport Method |
|---|---|---|
| Small tools, hand equipment | $0–$75 | UR service truck or customer pickup |
| Scissor lifts, small compressors | $75–$200 | Flatbed truck |
| Boom lifts (45–80 ft), skid steers | $150–$400 | Tilt-deck trailer |
| Large boom lifts (100+ ft), excavators | $300–$800 | Lowboy trailer |
| Trench boxes, large shoring | $250–$600 | Flatbed with boom truck |
| Generators (250+ kW) | $200–$600 | Lowboy or step-deck trailer |
| Portable offices / containers | $200–$500 | Tilt-bed or roll-off |

### 6.3 Fee Negotiation

- Standard delivery fees are set by the branch based on market rates.
- National Accounts may have negotiated delivery rates or delivery-free thresholds (e.g., free delivery for rentals over $X/month).
- Branch Managers have authority to waive or discount delivery fees for competitive situations or large orders.
- ISRs should check with the BM before waiving delivery fees.

## 7. Missing Key & Toll Transponder Charges

### 7.1 Missing Keys

- Equipment keys not returned with the equipment are charged at a flat rate.
- Typical charge: **$25–$75 per key** depending on key type (standard, electronic, or coded).
- Master keys for late-model equipment may cost more due to programming requirements.

### 7.2 Toll Transponder

- Some UR delivery/transport vehicles and towable equipment are equipped with toll transponders.
- If a customer incurs tolls while transporting UR equipment (e.g., on a customer-picked-up towable compressor), the toll charges are passed through to the customer.
- Unreturned or lost transponders are charged at **$25–$50** per transponder.

## 9. Invoice Dispute Resolution

### 9.1 Common Dispute Types

| Dispute Type | Typical Cause | Resolution Path |
|---|---|---|
| Billing period incorrect | Off-rent date discrepancy | Verify off-rent date in RentalMan; adjust if UR error |
| Rate disagreement | Customer expected different rate | Verify quote, contract, and NA rate schedule |
| Unauthorized charges | Customer unaware of ESC, FCC, or RPP | Explain charges per contract terms; adjust if error |
| Damage charge dispute | Customer disagrees with damage assessment | Review photos, RPP coverage, and branch inspection report |
| Duplicate billing | Same rental period invoiced twice | Credit memo for duplicate; investigate root cause |
| Delivery fee dispute | Customer expected free delivery | Check NA agreement or quote; adjust if applicable |

### 9.2 Dispute Resolution Procedure

1. **Customer contacts ISR or branch** with the invoice number and dispute details.
2. **ISR reviews** the contract, RentalMan notes, and relevant documentation (photos, signed agreements).
3. **If the dispute is valid** (UR error), ISR processes a credit memo in RentalMan:
   - Credit memos require Branch Manager approval for amounts over $500.
   - Credit memos over $2,500 require District Manager approval.
   - Credit memos over $10,000 require VP/Regional approval.
4. **If the dispute is not valid** (charges are correct), ISR explains the charges clearly with supporting documentation.
5. **If the customer escalates**, ISR involves the Branch Manager or Sales Manager.
6. **All dispute interactions are documented** in RentalMan contract notes.

### 9.3 Credit Memo Process

| Step | Action | System |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ISR identifies the need for credit adjustment | Review in RentalMan |
| 2 | ISR creates a Credit Memo Request in RentalMan | RentalMan → Credit Memo module |
| 3 | Reason code is selected (billing error, goodwill, rate adjustment, damage reversal, etc.) | RentalMan |
| 4 | Approver reviews and approves/denies | Per approval authority matrix |
| 5 | Credit memo is applied to the customer's account | RentalMan |
| 6 | Customer is notified of the adjustment | Email or phone |
| 7 | Credit appears on the next statement or as a standalone credit invoice | RentalMan billing cycle |

## 10. Credit Application Process

### 10.1 New Customer Credit Setup

1. Customer completes the United Rentals Credit Application.
   - Available online at unitedrentals.com or in PDF at any branch.
2. Required information:
   - Legal business name, DBA (if applicable), EIN / Tax ID
   - Business address, phone, email
   - Type of business (corporation, LLC, sole proprietor, partnership)
   - Years in business
   - Principals / owners (names, titles, SSN for personal guarantee if required)
   - Bank reference (institution name, account number, contact)
   - Trade references (minimum 3 — company name, contact, phone, account number)
   - Estimated monthly rental volume
   - Requested credit limit
3. Application is submitted to the UR Credit Department (centralized).
4. Credit review process:
   - Bank reference verification
   - Trade reference checks
   - D&B (Dun & Bradstreet) credit report pull
   - Personal credit check (if sole proprietor or personal guarantee required)
5. Decision timeline: **2–5 business days** from complete application submission.
6. Possible outcomes:
   - **Approved** — Account created with assigned credit limit and terms (typically Net 30)
   - **Approved with conditions** — Lower credit limit, personal guarantee required, or COD until established
   - **Denied** — Customer must rent on COD basis

### 10.2 Credit Limit Increases

- Existing customers can request credit limit increases.
- Request is submitted to the Credit Department with justification.
- Review considers payment history, account tenure, and current utilization.
- Typical processing time: 3–5 business days.

## 11. Late Payment Handling

### 11.1 Standard Terms

- Standard credit terms are **Net 30** (payment due 30 days from invoice date).
- National Accounts may have different terms per their Master Rental Agreement.

### 11.2 Late Payment Consequences

| Days Past Due | Action |
|---|---|
| 1–15 days | Automated reminder sent; account flagged in RentalMan |
| 16–30 days | Collections contact initiated (phone/email) |
| 31–60 days | Account placed on **credit hold** — no new rentals until payment received |
| 61–90 days | Formal demand letter sent; account escalated to senior collections |
| 90+ days | Account referred to third-party collections agency or legal action |

### 11.3 Credit Hold Impact

- When an account is on credit hold, **no new contracts can be created** in RentalMan.
- Existing active rentals continue (equipment is not pulled from the jobsite).
- Hold is released once past-due balance is paid.
- ISRs should check account status before creating contracts — RentalMan will display a credit hold warning.

### 11.4 Late Fees

- Late payment fees may apply per the terms on the rental agreement.
- Typical rate: **1.5% per month** (18% annually) on past-due balances.
- Late fees are automatically calculated by the billing system.

## 12. Tax Considerations

### 12.1 Sales/Use Tax

- Equipment rental is subject to sales tax in most states.
- Tax rates vary by state, county, and municipality.
- RentalMan automatically calculates applicable tax based on the jobsite address.
- Tax-exempt customers must provide a valid exemption certificate (state-specific form) before the transaction.

### 12.2 Tax Exemption

- ISR collects the tax exemption certificate from the customer.
- Certificate is uploaded to the customer's account in RentalMan.
- Once on file, tax is automatically exempted for future transactions.
- Exemption certificates must be renewed per state requirements (some states require annual renewal).
